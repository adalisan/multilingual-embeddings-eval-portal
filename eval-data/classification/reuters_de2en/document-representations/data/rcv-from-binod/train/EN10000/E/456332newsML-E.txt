Duff &amp; Phelps Credit Rating Co expects Mexico &apos;s inflation rate to decline to about 18 percent and GDP growth to reach 4.5 percent at yearend 1997 , according to a new Credit Analysis report on the country by the rating agency . 
Mexico &apos;s fiscal discipline and tight monetary policies successfully abated inflation and interest rates in 1996 , noted Duff &amp; Phelps vice president Andres O &apos;Bryne , author of the report . 
Duff &amp; Phelps Credit Rating currently rates Mexico &apos;s long-term foreign currency obligations Double B and believes the economic outlook for the country is stable . 
&quot; The economy &apos;s vulnerability to unexpected shocks has been considerably reduced , &quot; O &apos;Bryne said in the report . 
Duff &amp; Phelps also said it views Mexico &apos;s recent electoral reforms as positive . 
&quot; The improvement in Mexico &apos;s electoral system and overall political reform will benefit the country in the medium to long term , &quot; O &apos;Bryne added .
