Iraqi leader Saddam Hussein and his cabinet have discussed setting up a free-trade zone in southern Iraq , newspapers said on Wednesday . 
&quot; The cabinet ... discussed the establishment of a free trade and industry zone in Basra , &quot; the government newspaper al- Jumhouriya said , quoting a cabinet meeting chaired by Saddam . 
the paper gave no further details but it said the cabinet ratified the idea and asked the Iraqi Economic Affairs Committee to study its practical implementation . 
it said the zone would be set up near the Khour al-Zubair area where stores , communications , terminals and transport facilities were available . 
Iraq is under stringent U.N. trade sanctions imposed after its 1990 invasion of Kuwait .
