European Union foreign ministers failed to agree on Saturday on whether to provide military assistance to Albania , Spanish Foreign Minister Abel Matutes said . 
Matutes told reporters that Italy , Greece , Denmark and France had been in favour of military intervention of some sort to restore order in Albania . 
but he said Britain , Germany and Sweden had been vehemently opposed to such action . 
&quot; The majority is against , &quot; he added . 
diplomats said the ministers had discussed a number of proposals to deliver some sort of civil , political and economic assistance to Albania . 
the proposals included the option of setting up a bridgehead to allow aid to enter ports and the idea of sending police advisers .
