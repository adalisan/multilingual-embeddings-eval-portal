Australia was split into five time zones on Sunday when daylight saving started in some states and territories . 
clocks moved forward one hour in Victoria , New South Wales and the Australian Capital Territory on Sunday , putting the two states and territory 11 hours ahead of GMT . 
clocks also went forward an hour in South Australia , putting that state 10-1 / 2 hours ahead of GMT . 
the southern island state of Tasmania started daylight saving last month , putting it 11 hours ahead of GMT . 
the northern state of Queensland remains on its standard time 10 hours ahead of GMT , Western Australia remains eight hours ahead of GMT and the Northern Territory stays 9-1 / 2 hours ahead of GMT . 
it is up to individual Australian states and territories to decide whether or not to adopt summer time .
