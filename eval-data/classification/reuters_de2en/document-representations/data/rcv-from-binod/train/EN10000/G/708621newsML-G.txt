Egyptian prosecutors have agreed to send to court 23 suspected Islamic militants accused of planning violent attacks , security sources said . 
the suspects , alleged leaders of the militant Gama &apos;a al-Islamia ( the Islamic Group ) and its military wing , are accused of organising attacks targeting police , tourists , journalists , artists and ministers , they said late on Thursday . 
authorities seized weapons and money belonging to the group , all of whom were said to have returned from Sudan and received military training abroad . 
an alleged fugitive group leader , Mustafa Hamza , is among the accused . 
there was no date for the court hearing or arrests . 
Gama &apos;a has been fighting the government since 1992 to establish a strict Islamic state . 
more than 1,000 people have been killed in the conflict .
