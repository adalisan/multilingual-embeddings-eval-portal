CBOT wheat futures closed lower as hopes faded for an increase in wheat exports due to transportation problems in Canada , traders said . 
wheat rallied sharply late Tuesday on fund short covering tied to the traffic tie ups in Canada that led to ideas for a hike in quick shipments of U.S. wheat , mainly spring wheat . 
but Asian customers overnight indicated there probably would not be a noticable increase in U.S. wheat exports . 
Japan &apos;s Food Agency said it has no immediate plans to buy more U.S. wheat despite serious shipping delays at Canadian ports . 
Mickey Luth , director of research for the Chicago-based LaiFa Group said the Canadian problem may have given the market a boost but the rally was temporary and wheat futures will go back to test contract lows . 
traders said the wheat market gave a pacing glance to USDA &apos;s February supply / demand report . 
USDA said 1996-97 U.S. wheat carryout would total 474 million bushels , slightly above the 469 million bushel carryout forecast by USDA in its January report . 
wheat closed unchanged to 4-3 / 4 cents per bushel lower , with March down 4-3 / 4 at $ 3.57-1 / 4 a bushel . 
USDA said total 1996-97 U.S. ending stocks would be 474 million bushels , above the 469 million projected in January . 
the government increased to 43 million bushels its outlook for soft red winter ending stocks from the previous 28 million bushels . 
but the hard red winter ending stocks figure was trimmed to 144 million bushels from the previous 159 million bushels . 
traders said Cargill Inc and R.J. O &apos;Brien each sold two million bushels of March . 
Refco Inc , Iowa grain and LIT America each sold one million bushels of March , traders said . 
Chartists said first support in the March was $ 3.54-1 / 4 and second support was at $ 3.42-1 / 4 . 
the session low for March was $ 3.54 . 
first resistance was at $ 3.66-1 / 4 and second resistance was at $ 3.78-1 / 4 . 
the session high for March was $ 3.62 . 
the nine day relative strength index ( RSI ) for March closed at 38 percent . 
the market generally considers an RSI of 30 or less as an oversold market . 
wheat futures volume was estimated at 24,000 contracts , above the 23,392 traded Tuesday , according to the CBOT . 
in wheat options , Tradelink futures bought 250 May $ 3.70 calls at 7-1 / 2 cents , Cargill investor Services bought 200 March $ 3.60 calls at three cents and sold 200 March $ 3.70 calls at one cent . 
wheat options volume was estimated at 3,500 contracts , below the 3,799 traded Tuesday .
