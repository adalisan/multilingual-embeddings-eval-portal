Jakarta share prices closed higher on Wednesday on the back of bargain-hunting in selected big-capitalised stocks and secondliners , brokers said . 
the Jakarta composite index rose 2.60 points , or 0.48 percent , to 542.20 points . 
gainers led losers by 45 to 39 , with 53 counters unchanged . 
brokers said overall trading was moderate with more bargain hunters came in to floor , bought selected big-capitalised stocks which had discounted since Jakarta riot on July .

property firm Duta Anggada topped the active list with 15.6 million shares traded , rising 50 rupiah to 2,375 following continued market talk that the company had finalised a joint venture agreement with Singapore-based property firm .
