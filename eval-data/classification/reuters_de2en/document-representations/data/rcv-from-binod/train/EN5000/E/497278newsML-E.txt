Portugal &apos;s inflation , expressed as a year-on-year rate , was heading for levels in the second quarter of the year not seen since the 1960s , central bank governor Antonio de Sousa . 
de Sousa told a parliamentary committee that he saw no inflationary pressures in the economy for the current quarter . 
year-on-year inflation slowed to 2.5 percent in March from 2.9 percent in February and analysts have forecast a rate below two percent in April or May .
