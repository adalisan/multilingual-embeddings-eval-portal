contracts on the treasury bill repurchase market in Shanghai closed up across the board on Tuesday on a technical rebound after losses in the past few trading days , traders said . 
the annual rate for the most actively traded seven-day contract rose to 9.90 percent from 8.04 percent on Monday . 
total turnover on the six-contract market fell to 2.53 million lots from 4.72 million . 
one lot is 1,000 yuan face value of treasury bills . 
traders said the rates met support at low levels after a slower pace in issue of new shares pushed down the rates over the past few sessions . 
trading volume was not expected to expand because no new share issues were due in the next two days , they said . 
traders said they expected the rates to consolidate around current levels on Wednesday on a lack of market-moving news . 
contracts on the T-bill spot market ended mostly lower , mainly because unattractive yields made investors unwilling to buy , traders said . 
the most actively traded seven-year bond that matures in November 2003 was at 99.84 yuan from 99.90 yuan . 
combined volume of the eight-contract market fell sharply to 373,789 lots from 772,163 lots . 
volumes shrank sharply , indicating some short-term funds had flowed out of the spot market , traders said . 
traders said they expected spot rates to fall further in the short term .
