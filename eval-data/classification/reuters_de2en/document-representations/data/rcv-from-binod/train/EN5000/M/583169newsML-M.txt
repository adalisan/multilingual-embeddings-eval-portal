Indian metals &apos; prices on Wednesday were mixed as the trade awaited the announcement of selling prices by state-owned agencies for the second half of this month , dealers said . 
nickel and zinc fell while other base metals were steady . 
nickel prices dropped by 100 rupees at 36,700 rupees per quintal on demand resistance at higher levels while a setback in the world market prompted profit-taking . 
nickel was up by 200 rupees per quintal on Tuesday . 
zinc slid by 50 rupees at 7,850 rupees per quintal on improved arrivals after a gain of 50 rupees on Tuesday . 
copper was quoted steady at 13,550 rupees per quintal , aluminium at 7,525 rupees and lead at 4,150 rupees per quintal . 
tin was available at a previous level of 31,100 rupees per quintal but industrial buying remained slow .
