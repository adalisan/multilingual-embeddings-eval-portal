Greek shares slid 2.83 percent by the close on Friday as the market continued to suffer from higher interbank rates , adding to Thursday &apos;s 1.92 percent dip . 
but the one-month Athibor , the reference rate for Greece &apos;s interbank market , eased 16 basis points to 11.34 percent on Friday . 
Greece &apos;s central bank intervened again to send the drachma a notch higher against major currencies and draining surplus interbank liquidity . 
the central bank was estimated to have sold more than 1.2 billion ecus this week to shore up the drachma and pressure on the currency appeared to ease by the week &apos;s end . 
Forex forwards were trading lower and bond prices were up on increased demand , with offer prices dissapearing in some cases . 
market participants were not unnerved by tumbling prices on Friday , viewing the turmoil in interbank and forex markets as a good opportunity for a brief correction in equities , offering investors cheaper stock . 
&quot; Uneased forex and interbank markets provided equity prices with an opportunity to correct and raise liquidity ahead of ( the state telecom ) OTE &apos;s rights issue in June , &quot; said Petros Parisi , broker at Nuntius Securities . 
&quot; Having come down some 100 points in the course of one week , there are splendid buying opportunities around in a wide range of stocks , &quot; said Vasilios Kavvalos , an analyst at Omega Securities . 
prices were seen remaining rangebound in the near-term . 
&quot; Prices should consolidate between 1,600 and 1,680 points until rates ease back , &quot; Parisis said . 
as long as long-term rates do not increase and conditions similar to the May 1994 currency turmoil are not experienced there should be no concern , he added . 
investor interest was seen remaining high with buyers seeking lower prices . 
&quot; The flow of funds into equities has not been interrupted , but investors make sure that they purchase shares at lower prices , &quot; Parisis said .
