four Iraqi children were killed when a bomb dropped by Western forces in 1991 exploded in their village , al-Iraq newspaper reported on Thursday . 
the newspaper said the children , aged two to seven years , were killed in Sinna village in the Amarah area 370 km ( 230 miles ) south of Baghdad . 
three children were injured . 
the bomb was dropped by U.S.-led forces during the 1991 Gulf War to eject Iraqi troops from Kuwait , the newspaper said . 
Iraqi civil defence units were able to defuse two more bombs near the place where the incident took place , it added .
