Indian cotton prices were quiet at the lower level on Monday while Gujarat varieties suffered some fresh losses , dealers said . 
Punjab cotton met with some mill demand while Gujarat varieties extended losses on the back of rising new crop arrivals , one dealer said . 
in spot deals Punjab saw-ginned was steady at 1,475 / 1,550 rupees per maund ( 37.32 kg ) for the average variety and at 1,600 / 1,620 for the superior one . 
Bengal-deshi was unchanged at 840 / 870 . 
Long-staple Gujarat Sankar-4 fell by 400 / 500 to 17,000 / 17,500 and to 18,000 / 18,500 for both types . 
closing sentiment remained subdued as traders expected higher new crop arrivals to come in this week .
