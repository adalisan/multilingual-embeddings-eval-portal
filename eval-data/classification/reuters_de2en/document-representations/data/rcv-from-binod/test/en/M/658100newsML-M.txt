the Australian dollar tumbled and debt futures rose sharply after the Australian Bureau of Statistics reported that employment dropped by 40,300 in May , pushing the unemployment rate to 8.8 percent . 
forecasts had centred on a 15,000 employment rise with the unemployment rate staying at 8.7 percent . 
the dollar was quoted at US $ 0.7520 / 30 compared with US $ 0.7563 / 68 just before the data release , while June 10-year bond futures were 92.900 against a pre-data 92.775 . 
September bill futures also rose , moving to 94.79 from 94.67 .
