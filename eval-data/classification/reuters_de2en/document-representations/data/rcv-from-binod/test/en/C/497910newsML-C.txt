Germany &apos;s Bayerische Vereinsbank AG , a leader in eastern European debt issuance , said on Wednesday it would issue straight fixed two-year bonds in Poland , becoming the first foreign bank to do so . 
Vereinsbank said its Polish subsidiary , Vereinsbank Polska S.A. , would issue the bond worth 100 million zlotys , or 55 million marks . 
the bond will carry an annual coupon of 16 percent and have a two year maturity , Vereinsbank said , adding the yield would be around that of Polish government bonds , which currently yield around 20 percent . 
Vereinsbank Polska will jointly manage the issue with Polska Kasa Opieki S.A.
