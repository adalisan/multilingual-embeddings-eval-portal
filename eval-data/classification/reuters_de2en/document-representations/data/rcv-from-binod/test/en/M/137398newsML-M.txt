the Pakistani rupee ended 0.10 up at 42.60 to the dollar on the kerb on Wednesday amid profit-taking in dollars after a 7.86 percent devaluation of the rupee by the central bank , dealers said . 
on Tuesday , the State Bank of Pakistan moved the rupee to 40.1200 / 40.3206 to the dollar from 36.9700 / 37.1549 . 
dealers said the move prompted players to book profits , but demand for dollars was likely to keep the rupee stable . 
the premium on foreign exchange bearer certificates closed at 6.25 percent , compared to its previous close at 7.25 percent .
