Arabica futures in New York ended mostly lower with front month May losing 1.65 cents / lb to close at 214.90 cents / lb , while July gave up 10 points to close at 194.05 cents / lb . 
news that Colombia &apos;s truck drivers had gone ahead with a long-planned work stoppage briefly drove prices up to new life-of-contract highs . 
however , news that Brazil &apos;s port workers had ended their work stoppage after two days , coupled with nervousness ahead of May &apos;s first notice day on Tuesday , quickly put an end to the rally . 
today &apos;s weaker tone does not mean the end of the market &apos;s bullish tone , as supplies must be expected to remain tight . 
some analysts are firmly convinced , that the peak has not yet been reached and that further price rises are a definite possibility . 
after the close of trading , the CSCE reported a further fall of certified coffee stocks , which underlines current tight supplies .

Colombia - According to trade sources the strike by Colombia &apos;s main truck drivers &apos; union will affect deliveries against May contracts if it lasts more than ten days . 
the strike by the Colombian Truck Drivers &apos; Association ( ACC ) which represents about 80 per cent of Colombia &apos;s truckers , was called in March on grounds that the government , and private transportation companies , failed to fulfill terms of an accord that ended a crippling , 11-days strike in October . 
the latest strike comes as Colombia &apos;s coffee growers are just beginning to harvest the so-called &apos; mitaca &apos; crop , which accounts for about 40 per cent of yearly production . 
Brazil - Brazil trade house Tristao has raised its production estimate for 1996 / 97 to 27.5 MN bags , up from its previous forecast of 25.85 MN bags . 
production in 1997 / 98 is estimated at 23.5 MN bags . 
the company said that the drop in output is partly cyclical in nature . 
Netherlands - The Netherlands imported 252,956 bags of coffee in November 1996 , 2 per cent less than was imported the same month the previous year . 
that brought total imports since the beginning of 1996 up to 2.909 MN bags , 8.5 per cent more than was imported the same period in 1995 . 
total imports in calendar year 1995 dropped by 1.8 per cent to 2.910 MN bags . 
main suppliers of green coffee in January / November 1996 were Uganda ( 167,000 vs 199,000 bags ) , El Salvador ( 198,900 vs 89,000 bags ) , Brazil ( 144,100 vs 343,100 bags ) and Colombia ( 414,800 vs 393,000 bags ) . 
the country exported 529,100 bags of coffee in January / November 1996 , nearly 6 per cent less than was exported the same period in 1995 . 
total exports in calendar year 1995 dropped by 15 per cent to 616,900 bags .
