Brazil &apos;s real fell 0.07 percent to close at 1.0468 per dollar in the commercial foreign exchange market as banks held dollars in anticipation of a mini-devaluation later in the week , dealers said . 
&quot; February is a short month and next week is carnival . 
so we are expecting the Central Bank to set a mini-devalution by the end of this week , &quot; one dealer said . 
market players said the Central Bank would probably lower the mini-band by 0.05 percent to 0.10 percent by the end of the week . 
Tuesday , the Central Bank set a weaker mini-band for the currency at 1.0460 to 1.0510 reais . 
preliminary figures for commercial and financial foreign exchange contracts showed a Wednesday surplus of $ 188 million . 
in the interbank floating rate market , the real fell 0.01 percent to end at 1.0518 , according to dealers . 
in the illegal parallel market , the real rose 0.28 percent to end at 1.082 .
