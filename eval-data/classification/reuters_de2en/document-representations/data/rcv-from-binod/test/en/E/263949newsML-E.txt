the Colombian government said Wednesday the balance of payments deficit dropped to 5.3 percent of gross domestic product in 1996 . 
at a press conference on economic performance in 1996 and predictions for 1997 , the government forecast the balance of payments deficit would fall further to 5.2 percent of GDP in 1997 . 
the statements came shortly after a meeting of the National Council for Economic and Political Planning , which includes government ministers with an economic portfolio and the National Planning Department . 
he added that public sector net foreign debt would remain unchanged at 2.3 percent of GDP in 1996 and 1997 . 
foreign investment increased 19.5 percent through 1996 to total $ 2.6 billion , he said . 
the spokesman quoted the government as saying the trade deficit was 2.5 percent of GDP in 1996 and said the same figure was forecast for 1997 . 
in reference to the performance of key sectors of the economy , the spokesman said the value of parchment coffee production , a mainstay of the Colombian economy , fell 14.6 percent in real terms in 1996 , but that the government forecast a 3.5 percent increase in the value of parchment coffee production for 1997 .
