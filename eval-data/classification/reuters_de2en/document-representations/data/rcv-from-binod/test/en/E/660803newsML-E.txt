U.S. producer prices fell for the fifth straight month in May , dropping by 0.3 percent , the Labor Department said on Friday in a report that was likely to be greeted warmly on Wall Street . 
it was the first time in 45 years that wholesale prices have dropped for five consecutive months . 
the May decline followed a whopping 0.6 percent price drop in April . 
excluding volatile food and energy costs , producer prices fell by 0.3 percent last month , after a 0.1 percent drop in April .
