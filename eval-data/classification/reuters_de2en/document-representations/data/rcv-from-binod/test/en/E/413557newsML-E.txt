the Israeli government has approved bilateral agreements with nine countries to protect foreign investment , Israel &apos;s Treasury said on Sunday . 
the agreements , which define what constitutes foreign investment and set out a legal framework to settle claims , are with Uzbekistan , Albania , Ukraine , Argentina , Georgia , India , Turkey , Turkmenistan and Kazakhstan . 
the ministry said the agreements were aimed at encouraging Israeli investment into developing countries , particularly those of the former Soviet Union and Latin America . 
Avraham Talmor , the Treasury official who headed the team that negotiated the agreements , added that the accord would also foster joint investments in third countries . 
Israel already has similar agreements with 20 other developing countries , he said , and was in negotiations with about 20 others .
