French shares opened sharply lower on Thursday on the back of an overnight fall on Wall Street and ahead of key unemployment data in the United States on Friday . 
shares in Generale des Eaux and Havas were suspended pending a statement . 
the two companies are scheduled to hold a news conference at 1630 GMT where they are expected to announce a deal concerning their stakes in cable TV company Canal Plus . 
Canal Plus shares were down 1.45 percent at 1,155 francs . 
the bourse &apos;s blue-chip CAC-40 index was down 19.89 points or 0.78 percent at 2,521.36 at 0921 GMT while the CAC-40 futures contract fell to 2528 from 2546.5 at Wednesday &apos;s close . 
shares in chemicals group Rhone-Poulenc were down 3.04 percent after state agency Consortium de Realisation said it had sold a 4.6 percent stake in Rhone . 
Fund managers said the shares were placed at 177.50 francs each . 
shares in skincare and cosmetics firm Clarins were down 1.29 percent at 765 francs after the company announced a convertible bond issue totalling between 890 million and 1.02 billion francs .
